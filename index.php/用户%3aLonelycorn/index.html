Abstract

The 2010 Shanghai EXPO is the first Worlds’ Fair that China holds. Since the year 1851 London had held the “Great Exhibition of the Works of Industry of all Nations”, the worlds’ fair is now exerting its’ growing effects on the whole world on the communication of culture, the exhibition of high-techs, the collaboration spirits, and the coming future. The success in holding the 2010 Shanghai EXPO will greatly enhance China’s International status, as well as stimulate the economic development of the region of the Yangtze River Delta, thus improving the national comprehensive strength of China. Therefore it is of great significance to the prediction of China’s culture and economic development to build up mathematical model and make quantitative analysis of the influence of 2010 Shanghai EXPO.

The problem requires us to make quantitative analysis on a certain aspect of the world’s fair. Considering the booming of Internet during recent years and the popularization of computer science, people are now able to know the stories and news of the world even without stepping out their house. It is acceptable that the searching volume of the key words of a successful and influential EXPO should stay a relatively high and stable level during a considerable long period of time. So we decide to begin our analysis with the searching volume of key words.

Using the chart from the result of searching the key words “World’s Fair” on Google Trends, and combining with the information that the problem offered, we make a preliminary study due to the aspect of searching volume. And we’ve built mathematical models based on this information to make further analysis.

First of all, the growth of International Internet tends to have an exponential form with respect to time, which means a larger amount of searching volume with the identical amount of influence. Therefore the simple chart from Google Trends does not represent the influence of the (successive?) World’s Fair. Hence we need to set up the model of growth of International Internet, introduce the “Coefficient of Internet Growth” (CIG), and modify the searching volume of key words of World’s Fair according to this model.

2.3.3 Malthusian population Growth Equation

When Malthus was researching the statistical data of the population of the recent couple of centuries, he drew the conclusion that the increment of population in a unit of time is proportional to the amount of population. He set forth the exponential model of population growth in 1798.

basic presumptions of the model: the ratio of population growth is constant. That is to say, the increment of population in a unit of time is proportional to the amount of population at present.

Let N(t) represent the amount of population in year t, Then N(t+dt) is the amount in year t+dt. Despite that value of N(t) is integer, we re-define N(t) as a continuous and differentiable function so that we can use the powerful tool of Calculus, and we have

N(t+dt)-N(t) / dt=kN(t)

where k is the ratio of population growth. When dt → 0, we can transform the equation into

dN(t)/dt=kN(t)

let the initial condition at t=0 be N(0)=N0. Malthus drew his famous conclusion that population growth is exponential from equation (2.1). that's why equation (2.1) is called Malthusian population growth equation.

2.4 Status of Internet Growth

the status of Internet growth can be represented quantitatively in the following aspects:

number of Internet users

number of websites

number of domains

number of connected computers

number of IP addresses

each one of the five aspects has obvious influence on the others. For example, as the number of websites increases, the content of Internet becomes more abundant, which attracts more net users and thus result in the growth in Internet users; the growing of Internet users in return counts for the boost of websites and contents and connected computers (as infrastructure); the increasing of connected computers requires more IP addresses; etc.

according to the analysis above, we draw the preliminary conclusion that the current status of Internet growth has positive influence on the speed of Internet growth. Analyze data from a variety of sources, we see that in recent years the growth of Internet is kind of exponential, so we presume that the Internet is growing with an exponential trend.

To better describe the influence the current status of Internet growth has on the speed of development, we introduce the influence coefficient k.

On the presumption we made, we have the differential equation of IG-t

dIG/dt=kIG

that is just the model of population growth by Malthus. By solving the equation we get the general solution

IG=A*exp(kt)

we define A in the formula above as Prevalence Factor.

Particularly we need to address that the influence coefficient k is a constant that does not change with time. What's more, no matter which aspect of the five we choose to build the model, k should be the same constant. That is because all these models are for the same Internet, and k is decided exclusively by Internet itself.

What's more need to mention is there are some claws in the Malthusian model here. First of all, the Internet dates back to the ARPANET in 1969 but not the very beginning of time; the wide spread and development of Internet set out in the 1990s. Secondly, the period we are researching starts by Jan 2000. the classical Malthusian model does not apply to the two conditions here, so it does not fit for the growth of Internet.

To better describe the growth of Internet, we introduce the idea of Historical Factor. HF is a constant introduced to eliminate the error caused by the different starting point of time and has the same dimension as IG. Basically HF represent the status of Internet at the beginning (Jan 2000).

the revised Malthusian model is

IG=PF*exp(kt)+HF

3 Model of International Fairs(MIF)

3.1 Model of International Fairs

Before discussing the construction of the model of search data about world’s fair in detail, we can firstly consider building the search data model of “International Fairs”. As is known to all, the World’s Fairs, Olympic Games and World Cup called the world three international fairs. Through analysis of Google Trends, let’s see the search statistics of World’s Fair, Olympics Games and World Cup in recent years firstly.

CHART

According to the several data images above, it’s intuitive to find the common ground of these “International Fairs” in the information of search volume: during the time before and after those events took place, the search volume of these events would have a rapid change and reach the top when it was held. Simultaneously, we also find that the search volume chart of Olympic Games and World Cup are changed simply. Compared to those results, the search volume of World’s Fair is more complicated and fluctuant.

Actually, the Olympic Games and World Cup are all “Sports Fair”. They are of short duration and “high strength”. On the contrary, the World’s Fair, which is a fair of economy and culture, lasts half year. Except the opening and closing ceremony, it has no activities in high concentration. That is why the search volume chart of World’s Fair fluctuates over time, and its temporary crest would be time when the activities are concentrated. On this point, we will make a expatiation in the following data analysis.

Firstly, we start from the search volume chart of Olympic Games, which is simply. According to the principles of statistics, we can consider that the chart is approximately consistent with Gaussian distribution. Here are the theoretical bases:

Central Limit Theorem

Information Entropy Theorem

Maximum Entropy Theorem

By the above theoretical analysis and the observation of the image data, we have a prima facie that the search volume chart of Olympic Games and World Cup are consistent with Gaussian distribution. According to the actual data, we give the following search volume as a function of time:

Analyze the function

y0: It can be seen as a basic constant. It reflects the basic “Information Index” of the event, which is determined by the development of the Internet search volume and redundant data.

A:Peak. It reflects the maximum of the search volume of the event.

σ:It reflects the width of the peak in the Gaussian distribution, which is determined by the influential duration of the event basically.

It can be said that the above four parameters determine the features of the function of search volume, which can also reflect some features of the event. So, how do they determine the event influence?

Obviously, the A determines the peak, and the peak of the search volume is proportional to the concern level of the event. The influential duration of the event is also an evaluation parameter of the influence.
